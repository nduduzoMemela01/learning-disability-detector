About Dyslexia
What is Dyslexia?

Dyslexia is a specific learning disability that is neurological in origin. It is characterized by difficulties with accurate and/or fluent word recognition, poor spelling, and decoding abilities.

Common Symptoms
  • Difficulty reading aloud
  • Slow reading speed
  • Trouble with spelling
  • Letter reversals (b/d, p/q)
  • Difficulty following written directions
  • Trouble with word problems in math
Myths vs Facts
Myth Fact
Dyslexia is a vision problem Dyslexia is a brain-based learning difference
People with dyslexia see letters backwards They have trouble connecting letters to sounds
Dyslexia is linked to low intelligence Dyslexia occurs at all intelligence levels
Dyslexia can be outgrown It's lifelong but strategies can help
